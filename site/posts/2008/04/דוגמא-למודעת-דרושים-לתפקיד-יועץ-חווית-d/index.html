במקרה הזה מדובר באחד מערוצי הטלוויזיה המסחריים הגדולים בבריטניה והגדרת התפקיד היא ארכיטקט מידע. ארכיטקטורת מידע נכונה וטובה (תוכן, חלוקה, ניווט, טרמינולוגיה, עימוד וכו') היא נדבך מרכזי מחווית שימוש חיובית ומספקת וכפי שניתן להתרשם מהנקודות השונות, מדובר במקרה הזה בתפקיד מקיף שכולל פעילויות מחקר, איפיון ואבחון שונות. יוצא לי להתקל במודעות מהסוג הזה על בסיס יומי כמעט בקבוצת משתמשים ביאהו! בשם london_usability ולדעתי המודעה הבאה משקפת בצורה מקיפה את דרישות התפקיד בתחום. כל אלה הן חלק ממתודולוגיית עיצוב מכוון-משתמש אותה תיארתי פה בעבר.
Channel 4 have a perm vacancy for a mid-level Information Architect. This is a fantastic opportunity to work in-house in a very creative environment with lots of lovely, friendly people. The job spec is as follows: Information Architect Channel 4 New Media is part of the New Media and Technology division within the Channel 4 Television Corporation. Channel 4 New Media is responsible for the new media output across the Channel 4 Group. New Media’s objective is to grow revenues and profits tenfold by 2012 together with producing innovative and challenging new media services in line with the Channel 4 ethos of 'doing it first, making trouble and inspiring change'. The primary platforms upon which New Media operates are online and mobile. The online activities encompass the websites channel4.com and e4.com, syndication of content across 3rd party websites and include a diverse range of projects from fourdocs, a user generated documentary site, to 4car, a leading UK car information site to Big Brother, the leading UK reality TV support website. Mobile activities include channel4 and e4 mobile wap sites and ShortCuts, a made for mobile TV channel, available on Vodafone and Orange networks. Job Purpose Specialist in user-centred design who will producer user experience solutions for Channel 4 New Media projects that fulfil the needs of users and help the department achieve our creative, brand and business aspirations. Key Responsibilities
  • To complete New Media projects that adhere to the highest standards of user experience and usability, and meet our accessibility and Search Engine Optimisation guidelines.
  • To work autonomously on assigned projects. This may include responsibility for selecting and briefing agencies and managing their output. The Information Architect must be able to scope and plan their own work, with the support of the Senior Information Architect.
  • Assisting other team members in their understanding of user-centred design, Accessibility and Search Engine Optimisation.
  • Effectively collaborate with the visual design team particularly, but also with multi-disciplinary teams including project managers, product managers, business analysts, editors and developers.
  • To research and prepare designs for new media products to help the audience find and use our content.
  • To proactively pinpoint challenges and difficulties with existing systems and suggest possible solutions
  • To re-purpose existing information and content elements, basic functions and templates as required
  • To incorporate appreciation and knowledge of usability, navigation and accessibility issues into IA project work
  • To offer expert advice to visual design, editorial and technical teams on design principles
Essential Skills and Competencies
  • Bachelor's or Master’s degree in information design, information science, HCI, technical writing or related field (or equivalent experience)
  • Organised and analytical with excellent attention to detail and able to understand and contribute to the creative process
  • Experience in analysing the usability of existing sites and making recommendations for improvements
  • Experience in developing user scenarios and personas
  • Experience of creating sitemaps,wireframes and functional specifications suitable for the technical team to build from
  • Experience in planning and running user research techniques such as interviews, card sorting and usability testing, and analysing the results of these to create actionable recommendations
  • Commitment to and thorough understanding of accessibility requirements and a thorough understanding of Search Engine Optimisation
  • Excellent written communication skills with an understanding of how to structure formal documentation
  • Excellent problem solving skills
  • Excellent stakeholder management skills
  • Ability to prioritise and plan own work with respect to deadlines and other constraints
  • A creative and lively approach to work
  • Ability to work well in a team
